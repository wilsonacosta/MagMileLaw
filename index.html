MICHAEL BALOURDOS

What's my goal? To get deals done. Nothing more. Many lawyers are notorious for 'killing' deals due to their overzealous representation of their clients. They tend to bog down negotiations with unnecessary arguments and take stubborn positions. Not me.

I strive to close out transactions without sacrificing crucial deal points for my clients. How? Well, because I wear a developer's 'hat' on many occasions, I'm able to understand the deal from both sides. As an active partner in a local, successful commercial real estate development firm, I can confidentially claim that I'm able to see through the eyes of my clients. Not many lawyers can do that. I can.

Since I constantly communicate and deal with my tenants, I know when to back off and when to squeeze. Real world, business experience gives me the edge over other lawyers to evaluate the situation and adapt to the existing environment and not simply use the same 'cookie cutter' legal tactics. When you personally fight the battles, you're better equipped to represent your client.

MARIO IVELJIC

As a Yale University graduate and accomplished soccer player, I was very accustomed to high pressure situations. So, when it came time to decide on a professional career, I did not shy away from the challenge of practicing law or the responsibilities of representing clientele all across the country.

16 years later, I have had the pleasure of representing a wide variety of clients â€“ from Fortune 500 companies to local businesses to individual clients. In doing so, I have focused on a very simple, commonsense approach to how I handle cases. I don't B.S. my clients. I don't tell them what they want to hear. I don't make any promises that I know I can't keep. Rather, I provide them with a thorough work up of their file. I discuss with them all the strengths and weaknesses of their case. As a former defense attorney with years of experience representing corporate clients and insurance companies, I warn my clients about how the other side will defend their case. I explain what their options might be, what possible results they might be able to obtain, and what path to take to obtain the best possible result. I then guide my clients on that path, and, throughout the process, I treat judges and opposing counsel with courtesy, professionalism and respect.

STEVEN MIKUZIS

I believe litigation is the last option. If possible, I always first calmly seek affable compromise in any dispute. But when compromise fails, I strike and strike hard to achieve my client's goals.

I first chaired my first 12 person jury trial as a first year associate at a large Chicago law firm. As the trial approached, the senior partner on the case became ill and was unable to appear. With no trial experience and little legal experience, I obtained a successful verdict for my client. My lack of legal experience at the time was shored up by my ability to connect with 12 diverse citizens of Cook County.

Since that case I have tried multiple cases to verdict and appeal; sometimes representing the plaintiff and sometimes the defendant. I have tried cases involving personal injury, breach of contract, business disputes & fraud. I have tried cases against lawyers from one-man shops, national recognized law firms and legends of my profession. As my knowledge base has grown with these experiences, I have come to realize one thing: when trial comes you want me in that courtroom. You want me by your side. You want me to explain to the trier of fact why you were wronged and how they can make it right.

GEORGE XAMPLAS

I believe success starts and ends with knowing who I represent, why I am representing them, and what it is we are up against. To me, this means more than just the facts-it means understanding the person-top to bottom, inside and out. My clients come from all walks of life and all different socio-economic circles, from Fortune 100 CEOs to person's living in Chicago's homeless shelters. But one thing they all share is the need for help and the need to be heard.

Born and raised in Chicagoland and growing up working in my family's small businesses, I learned fist hand the important life lessons of hard work and the vital need to listen and be fully engaged with the client. Add to this the fact that my formal education, anchored in science and the law, is as varied as it comes, I provide personal and professional legal services that meet and exceed my diverse clientele's highest expectations.

My practice is international. I have successfully represented clients from six continents on matters ranging from litigation to real estate and have experienced an enormous amount of legal issues over my career. However, the most important thing I have learned is that behind each and every case is an unique individual whose backgrounds and life experiences must be fully considered and taken into account when I work on their case.
